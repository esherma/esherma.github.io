About

Currently, I'm a Senior Research Scientist & Engineer at Credo AI, where I am the Tech Lead responsible for building the AI service and serve on our internal AI Governance Council.

Before industry, I earned my PhD in Computer Science from Johns Hopkins University, where I proved several novel results in theoretical causal inference related to non-IID settings and applied machine learning to improve diagnosis in cardiac surgery and mental health. My research has been recognized with a Google PhD Fellowship and published at top venues including NeurIPS, UAI, and AISTATS.

I care about working on things that matter: from responsible AI and AI safety, to healthcare, and equitable and efficient resource allocation. I'm drawn to 0‚Üí1 problems: building new capabilities, shaping product direction, and turning ideas into systems that create real impact. Technical depth is the means; pro-social impact is the goal.

Outside of work, I am principal cellist of the Hopkins Chamber Orchestra and am developing an obsession for road cycling. I also write the Grandparents' Guide to AI, a newsletter translating AI progress for normies.
